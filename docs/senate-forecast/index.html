Model

Hierarchical Bayesian election forecasting model for the 2026 U.S. Senate (35 races), estimated via Stan HMC (Hamiltonian Monte Carlo). Constructs a fundamentals-based prior per race from recency-weighted presidential lean, generic congressional ballot (GCB), presidential approval, incumbency advantage, and candidate quality. Polls are incorporated via hierarchical Stan model with Student-t poll likelihood (robust to outliers), national and regional random effects, and jointly estimated poll bias and degrees of freedom. Partial pooling across states within 4 regions borrows strength from related races. Uncertainty is propagated through 50,000 Monte Carlo draws from the Stan posterior, plus three forward-looking drift layers: national GCB drift (fat-tailed t-distribution), regional correlated drift, and state-level idiosyncratic drift. Drift magnitudes are time-dependent, scaling by √(months to election / 8). Parameters are optimized via leave-one-cycle-out cross-validation on 8 historical cycles (2010–2024), minimizing Winkler score conditional on ≥90% CI coverage.

Prediction intervals are constructed using split conformal inference on backtest residuals (actual margin minus posterior mean across all 8 historical cycles). Four variants are computed: (A) global split conformal (single 90% quantile across all calibration races), (B) stratified Mondrian conformal (separate quantiles for polled/unpolled × incumbent/open strata), (C) locally-adaptive conformal (race-specific intervals from K=30 nearest calibration races in feature space: partisan lean, polling availability, seat type, elasticity), and (D) recency-weighted conformal (exponential decay weights favoring recent cycles, τ=8 years). Coverage is validated via leave-one-cycle-out: the conformal quantile is trained on all cycles except the held-out year, then tested on the held-out year, yielding honest finite-sample coverage guarantees without distributional assumptions.

Data Sources

  • Senate polls: New York Times / 538 polling database
  • Generic ballot: Silver Bulletin GCB model (daily estimate)
  • Historical polls (backtest): NYT Upshot (2010–2012), RealClearPolitics (2014–2016), 538 historical Senate polls (2018–2024)
  • Fundamentals: Presidential election results (MIT Election Lab), FRED unemployment data, Silver Bulletin presidential approval
